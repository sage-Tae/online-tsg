## 1. Narrative Arc of Revised Paper

### 1.1 Previous narrative (to be revised)

"We prove r > r** ⇒ Core empty and k < n-1 ⇒ Core safe. These fully
characterize stability."

**Issue**: Pretends Theorem 11 + Corollary 16 together tile the space,
but ignores the 7 main-study (NN) instances where Theorem 11 is vacuous
yet the Core is empty — i.e., the **near-complement mechanism**.

### 1.2 Revised narrative

"We identify **three distinct mechanisms** affecting Core stability:

1. **Complement-coalition mechanism** (Theorem 11): r > r** ⇒ Core empty.
   Applies in the saturated regime (k = n, feasible complements).
   Certifies 14 of the 21 empty Cores in our main grid.

2. **Structural obstruction** (Corollary 16): k < n-1 ⇒ complement
   mechanism blocked. Empirically 100% Core-safe in our grid
   (79 + 16 = 95 instances).

3. **Near-complement mechanism** (NEW, §6.4 Remark): Even when Theorem 11
   is vacuous, the Core can be empty via simultaneous binding of
   complement (size n-1) and near-complement (size n-2) constraints.
   Accounts for the remaining 7 main-study empties and is empirically
   certified at Pattern A seed 123 for n ∈ {20, 30, 50} via restricted
   Core LP.

An analytic tight characterization of mechanism 3 is left open."

### Section 6.4 (Corollary 16 verification) — REWRITE

New wording:

> "Partitioning the 175 instances by peak queue regime (NN policy)
> yields three strata:
>   - k < n-1:  79 instances, all with nonempty Core (100%).
>   - k = n-1:  16 instances, all with nonempty Core (100%).
>   - k = n:    80 instances, 59 with nonempty Core (73.8%).
>
> The structural obstruction of Corollary 16 holds without exception:
> whenever k < n-1, the Temporal Core is nonempty. All empty Cores
> occur in the saturated regime k = n, consistent with the requirement
> that at least one complement coalition N\{i} be feasible.
>
> Within the k = n regime, Theorem 11 accounts for most but not all
> empty Cores. Of the 21 empty-Core instances, **14** satisfy r > r**
> and are certified by Theorem 11. The remaining **7** satisfy r ≤ r**;
> Theorem 11 is vacuous yet the Core is empty. This gap — between the
> complement-coalition sufficient condition and actual Core emptiness —
> is examined at scale in §6.8 and further below."

ADD subsection or box §6.4.1 (Near-complement mechanism):

> **Near-complement mechanism (empirical).** At (Pattern A, n=30, seed 123),
> we observe r = 1.038 ≤ r** = 1.088, so Theorem 11 does not fire. Yet a
> restricted Core LP over 10,000 sampled coalitions (all singletons, all
> N\{i}, and random intermediate-size coalitions) is infeasible with
> optimal ε* = 0.66 > 0, certifying Core emptiness. The binding
> constraints include coalitions of size n-1 (complement) AND size n-2
> (near-complement), simultaneously. The same pattern holds at n ∈ {20,
> 50} with seed 123 (ε* = 0.77 and ε* = 12.4 respectively, binding sizes
> [n-2, n-1] in each case). This contrasts with a clean Theorem 11 case
> (n=30, seed 42, r > r**) where only size-(n-1) constraints bind. We
> conjecture that a tight sufficient condition involves balanced
> collections of size-(n-1) and size-(n-2) coalitions; see §8.
